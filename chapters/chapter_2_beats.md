# Chapter 2 — A New Normal

## Chapter Beats

### Beat 1: 
Sunlight streams through the gaps in the blinds, painting stripes across the worn wooden floorboards of the living room. Daniel lies on the sofa, propped up by pillows, a faded throw blanket pulled loosely around his shoulders. The air is thick with the scent of chamomile tea and the faint, comforting aroma of Sarah’s baking – a cinnamon-raisin loaf she's been perfecting for weeks. A clock on the mantelpiece ticks rhythmically, each click a tiny hammer blow against the stillness. He watches the dust motes dance in the sunbeams, their slow pirouettes mirroring the sluggishness in his limbs. 

### Beat 2: 
Footsteps on the stairs announce Sarah’s arrival. She enters the room carrying a steaming mug, a worn copy of Jane Austen tucked under her arm. Her hair is pulled back in a loose braid, tendrils escaping to frame her face, dusted with flour. She sets the mug on the coffee table beside him, a smile softening the lines etched around her eyes. "Thought you might like a refill," she says, her voice low and soothing. "And maybe a little company."  She settles beside him on the sofa, the familiar weight of her body a comforting anchor against the constant tide of fatigue. 

### Beat 3: 
Daniel takes a tentative sip, the warmth spreading through his chest. "Thank you," he murmurs, the words catching in his throat. He clears his throat. "The doctor's words...they kept circling around in my head. 'No cure yet.'  He said it like a passing storm, but it feels like an earthquake, you know?"  Sarah places her hand over his, her fingers tracing gentle circles on the back of his hand. "I know, honey," she says quietly. "It's a lot to take in. But it doesn't mean it's the end of everything.  Dr. Bennett said it's manageable." 

### Beat 4: 
A wave of weariness washes over Daniel, leaving him feeling drained. He closes his eyes, the familiar ache in his head intensifying. He opens them again, his gaze falling on a worn leather notebook tucked in the corner of the coffee table. He reaches for it, his fingers brushing against the faded inscription on the cover – “Daniel’s Dreams” – written in his father’s looping handwriting. He hasn't touched this notebook in months, not since the world began to shrink around him. He runs a thumb over the inscription, a flicker of something akin to hope igniting in his chest. He opens the notebook, the paper whispering softly beneath his fingertips. He takes a deep breath, the faint scent of cinnamon and old paper mingling in the air. He picks up his pen.
